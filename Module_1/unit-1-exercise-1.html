Module 1 - Exercise 1 (John Deere)

The Old Deere

In fields of gold and skies so clear,
An old John Deere, year after year.
Rust on the hood, no cab to shield,
Yet still it toils in every field.

Aged by time, but strong at heart,
Its engine hums, a work of art.
Through sun and rain, it ploughs the ground,
A faithful friend, so steadfast, sound.

With every turn, a story told,
Of seasons past and futures bold.
Though paint may fade, its spirit stays,
In every furrow, it leaves its praise.

Rusty and worn, yet never still,
An old John Deere with iron will.
It works the land, from dawn till dusk,
A timeless bond, in dust and musk.
